### Working title

Slack, Zoom, and Meeting Fatigue: Practical Tactics for High-Velocity Teams.[^3]

### Core message

Back-to-back calls and constant Slack pings degrade focus; simple structural and behavioral changes‚Äînotification tuning, async norms, calendar buffers, and RAPID‚Äîreduce fatigue and restore deep work time.[^3]

### Audience and length

- AI2-Compute engineers, leads, managers; new hires in Slack-heavy culture; 500‚Äì700 words, 2‚Äì3 minute read.[^3]

- ‚ÄúIf the day feels like sprinting from Zoom to Slack and back, it‚Äôs not just in the head‚Äîthis environment taxes attention and energy, especially at AI2-COM‚Äôs pace‚Äù.[^2][^3]
- Name the pattern: constant pings + back-to-back meetings = context switching, nonverbal overload, and decision debt.[^8][^13][^2]


### Slack overload: why it drains

- Real-time threads, channel noise, and frequent context switching raise cognitive load and fatigue, reducing deep work windows.[^13][^14][^2]
- Multitasking/switching costs are well-documented; switching-time costs increase with task complexity and erode efficiency, supporting batching/check-ins vs. constant monitoring.[^14][^13]


### Slack tactics (3‚Äì5 quick wins)

- Tune notifications to DMs, mentions, project keywords; use DND during focus blocks; create a small VIP list for truly urgent people.[^2]
- Channel hygiene: star/pin essentials, mute/leave low-value channels; use Saved Items to batch review; schedule check-ins hourly or at set times.[^2]
- Promote async: concise updates, Slack Clips/video snippets, clear expectations that Slack isn‚Äôt for emergencies (use call/text instead).[^2]


### Zoom fatigue: evidence and fixes

- Stanford‚Äôs Bailenson identifies nonverbal overload drivers: intense eye gaze, always-on self-view (mirror anxiety), reduced mobility, and extra effort to send/parse cues; these factors predict ‚ÄúZoom fatigue‚Äù and have validated follow-on research (ZEF scale).[^9][^11][^8]
- Calendar buffers matter: Microsoft EEG research shows breaks between meetings reduce stress and improve engagement; Outlook defaults can auto-shortening meetings for built-in buffers.[^10][^12][^15]


### Zoom tactics (4‚Äì6 practical moves)

- Question video-by-default; prefer audio-only or async updates when stakes are low; try walking meetings when appropriate.[^2]
- Schedule meeting-free blocks mid-week; default to starting at :05/:35 to insert a buffer; instruct on Outlook setting (reference screenshot slot).[^4][^2]
- Hide self-view and shrink/move the window to reduce cognitive load; avoid multitasking during calls to prevent switching costs.[^8][^13][^2]
- Insert 5‚Äì10 minute breaks, especially between decision-heavy sessions; normalize quick camera-off rests with context.[^12][^2]


### Meeting overload and decision debt

- Unstructured decision ownership multiplies meetings and rework; legacy RACI struggles in fast-moving, cross-functional orgs like OCI Compute.[^5]
- RAPID clarifies roles up front‚ÄîRecommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide‚Äîreducing meeting count and sharpening agendas/outcomes; this was proposed as a leadership standard but underused.[^6][^7][^5]
- Internal sentiment: ‚ÄúNothing has changed. Still endless meetings.‚Äù reinforces the need for systemic adoption, not just personal tactics.[^7][^6]


### RAPID essentials to highlight

- R: Who drafts the proposal; A: Who must agree; P: Who executes; I: Who provides inputs; D: Who decides; scale the template to complexity; enforce for leader-engaged workstreams.[^5]
- Coaching from the top and Chiefs of Staff enforcement were part of the deployment plan; decider-oriented meetings should require a RAPID matrix and pre-alignment.[^5]


### Establishing rhythms

- Time-block daily: 90-minute deep work blocks protected by DND; 2‚Äì3 short communication blocks for Slack/Email/Approvals; end-of-day full logoff to prevent spillover.[^2]
- Team norms: Slack not for emergencies; Zoom optional for purely informational updates; choose the right tool‚ÄîSlack for quick updates, Zoom for discussion, email for non-urgent depth.[^2]


### ‚ÄúTry this next week‚Äù

- Default meetings to start at :05 with 25/50-minute durations for automatic breaks; document how to set Outlook defaults.[^4][^12]
- Pilot RAPID on one workstream; require a one-page RAPID matrix before any leadership meeting.[^5]

## Writing Style and Requirements

### Use Human Language - Rik's Style
- Write in a natural, conversational tone that feels authentic and human
- Use simple, direct language - avoid jargon and overly complex sentences
- Write as Rik would: clear, practical, and grounded in real engineering experience
- Keep it accessible - technical but not academic

### Simplicity
- Prefer simple words and short sentences over complex constructions
- Break down complex ideas into digestible pieces
- Use examples and analogies to make abstract concepts concrete
- Avoid unnecessary complexity - clarity is more important than sophistication

### Use References - Don't Hallucinate
- **Always ground your claims in the reference materials provided**
- When referencing concepts, ideas, or frameworks, cite the specific documents where they appear
- If you're not certain about a fact or detail, don't make it up - either omit it or clearly indicate uncertainty
- Synthesize from multiple sources rather than inventing new information
- When drawing from the draft blog posts (chatgpt.md, perpetuity.md, etc.), acknowledge the synthesis but make it your own
- Use explicit references to source documents when making specific claims (e.g., "As noted in the Compute AI Offsite document..." or "Building on the frameworks discussed in...")

## 5. Historical Lessons: Missed Opportunities

### The BPO vs. R&D Choice

Since the early 2000s, Mauritius embarked on developing the services sector with focus on Financial Services and ICT. In the wake of the new millennium, the country moved to promote high value-added services such as the ICT/BPO sector.

**What Mauritius prioritized:** BPO operations focusing on "do it cheaper" rather than innovation
**What was missed:** Building R&D centers and innovation hubs during the 2000s tech boom

### Dot-Com Era (Late 1990s - Early 2000s)

The 1990s called for development of the financial services sector, and Mauritius firmly established itself as a sound financial services jurisdiction. Since the early 21st century, Mauritius sought to develop its ICT industry to make it one of the pillars of the economy.

**Missed opportunity:** While focusing on traditional financial services, Mauritius missed the chance to become an early fintech hub or e-commerce platform center.

### Manufacturing Evolution

The 1980s were dominated by the textile boom; the 1990s were led by tourism and financial services; the 2000s saw the advent of ICT/BPO-real estate.

**What worked:** Export Processing Zones (EPZ) for textiles
**What was missed:** 
- Transitioning to electronics manufacturing when Asian tigers did
- Not developing semiconductor or component manufacturing capabilities
- Missing the solar panel and battery manufacturing wave

### Late Digital Infrastructure Development

Mauritius ranks 110th worldwide with an average cost of $50.59 for broadband connectivity, representing an increase from 2019. The Data Technology Park at Côte d'Or announced in Budget 2020-2021 was still unrealized a year later.
